# Taxonomy and Escalation Rubric — v3

## Categories

* **Incorrect account filing** - the status of a customers account has been set incorrectly

* **Lack of action in a reasonable timeframe** - no responses, no actions taken after commited to verbally or in writing, no responses or action after follow ups

* **Closed dispute without resolving the issue** - action was taken, but the problem wasn't addressed or solved

* **Unable to make contact through established channels** - the customer is unable to contact the organisation through the channels advertised by the brand

* **Disputed/unauthorized account** - the customer has disputed the status of their account

## Escalation Risk Signals

### Low - slight inconvenience for the customer

* Lack of or delayed responses

**Low example:** It appeared on my credit report that I had accidentally missed a payment. I paid it immediately and should be fixed. Hoping to remove this from my credit please.

### Medium - significant gap between the quality expectation and the delivered service

* Subpar service quality
* Speculative/conditional harm

**Medium example:** I sent a formal debt validation letter via Certified Mail on \<date\> to Portfolio Recovery Associates regarding an account ending in XXXX. This account is appearing on my credit report and I am disputing its validity and accuracy. As a debt buyer, Portfolio Recovery Associates must provide full chain of ownership documentation. I am requesting validation per my rights under the FDCPA and FCRA, or immediate deletion from all credit reporting agencies.

*This case is Medium because the consumer is disputing validity and referencing their rights, but no concrete harm has occurred yet. Speculative or conditional harm caps at Medium.*

### High - loss of money, time, reputation or health has occurred or is imminent

* Rights violations
* Abusive behaviour
* Financial hardship
* Credit score damage

## Tiebreaker Rule

If a complaint falls between Medium and High, move up to High **only if concrete harm has plausibly already occurred** (money lost, credit score damaged, job or housing affected, documented abusive contact). Speculative or conditional harm — "this could hurt my credit" — stays at Medium.
